Rohit Sharma also known as the Hitman of Cricket

About the Hitman Rohit Sharma

1.Birth: Rohit Gurunath Sharma was born on April 30, 1987, in Nagpur, Maharashtra, India.
2.Early Life: He grew up in Mumbai and started playing cricket at a young age.
3.Domestic Debut: Rohit made his First-Class debut for Mumbai in the 2006-07 season.
4.ODI Debut: He made his One Day International (ODI) debut for India on June 23, 2007, against Ireland.
5.Test Debut: Rohit Sharma's Test debut came on November 6, 2013, against the West Indies.
6.T20 International Debut: His T20 International debut came on September 19, 2007, against England.
7.Style of Play: Rohit is an elegant right-handed batsman known for his ability to play big innings.
8.Double Century in ODIs: On November 2, 2013, Rohit became the first cricketer to score a double century in ODI cricket. He scored 209 runs against Australia.
9.Most Double Centuries: As of September 2021, Rohit Sharma holds the record for the most double centuries in ODIs, achieving three of them (209, 264, and 208*).
10.Fastest T20 Century: In 2017, he scored a blistering century off just 35 balls in a T20 International against Sri Lanka, making it the fastest century in T20I cricket at that time.
11.Captaincy: Rohit has led the Indian cricket team as captain in various formats on multiple occasions.
12.ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year: He was awarded the prestigious Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for the ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year in 2020.
13.IPL Success: Rohit has been an integral part of the Mumbai Indians (MI) IPL franchise and has led the team to multiple IPL titles.
14.IPL Records: As of September 2021, he held the record for the most IPL titles won as captain and the most runs as an opening batsman in IPL history.
15.Most Runs in a World Cup: During the 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup, Rohit Sharma scored 648 runs, making him the tournament's leading run-scorer.
16.Most Centuries in a World Cup: In the same 2019 World Cup, Rohit hit five centuries, setting a new record for the most centuries in a single edition of the tournament.
17.Batting Records: Rohit Sharma holds various records, including being the fastest Indian to reach 2000, 3000, 4000, 5000, 6000, and 7000 runs in ODI cricket.
18.Versatility: Rohit is known for his adaptability in different formats, with a natural ability to switch gears and excel in both limited-overs cricket and the longer format.
19.Personal Life: Rohit is married to Ritika Sajdeh, and they have a daughter named Samaira.
20.Sportsmanship: He is highly regarded for his sportsmanship and leadership qualities both on and off the field.
